# (Laptop) Display Types
## LCD (Acronym)
- Liquid Crystal Display
## TN LCD (Acronym)
- Twisted Nematic Liquid Crystal Display
## IPS LCD (Acronym)
- In Plane Switching Liquid Crystal Display
## VA LCD (Acronym)
- Vertical Alignment Liquid Crystal Display
## OLED (Acronym)
- Organic Light Emitting Diode
## LED (Acronym)
- Light Emitting Diode
## LCD
- light shines through liquid crystals
- Advantages:
- Lightweight
- relatively low power
- relatively inexpensive
- Disadvantages:
- black levels are a challenge
- requires a separate backlight (such as florescent or LED)
## TN LCD
- original LCD technology
- fast response times
- poor viewing angles (color shifts)
## IPS LCD
- excellent color representation
- more expensive than TN LCD
## VA LCD
- "middleground" LCD
- good color representation
- slower response times than TN LCD
## OLED
- organic compound emits light when receiving an electric current
- Advantages:
- thinner and lighter than LCD (commonly used for mobile devices)
- no backlight needed
- very accurate color representation
- Disadvantages:
- costlier than LCD
## fluorescent backlighting
- LCD backlighting type
- often seen on older laptops
- uses an inverter to convert DC power to AC power
- CCFL (Cold Cathode Fluorescent Lamp)
- high voltage and power needed
- very thick
## LED backlighting
- LED backlit LCD
- contains a matrix of LEDs behind the screen and LEDs surrounding the edge of the screen
